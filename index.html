Te-Puia

A place that changes you.

About

Te Puia, located in Rotorua, New Zealand, is a cultural and geothermal destination that showcases both Maori traditions and the natural wonders of the Te Whakarewarewa geothermal valley. It's home to the New Zealand Maori Arts and Crafts Institute, where visitors can witness traditional carving and weaving, and it also features the famous Pōhutu Geyser, bubbling mud pools, and native kiwi birds.

Te Puia, specifically the New Zealand Māori Arts and Crafts Institute within it, has been around since 1963, when it was established by the NZ Māori Arts & Crafts Institute Act. This makes it 61 years old in 2024. It was created to preserve and promote Māori arts and culture.

What We Offer:

Geothermal Wonders:

Whakarewarewa Thermal Valley:

Explore this valley with its geysers, hot springs, and mud pools.

Pōhutu Geyser:

Witness the largest active geyser in the Southern Hemisphere, erupting up to 20 times a day.

Boiling Mud Pools:

See the bubbling and steaming mud pools that are a characteristic feature of the area.

Wildlife:

Kiwi Conservation Centre:

Observe New Zealand's national bird, the kiwi, in a specially designed habitat.

Māori Culture:

New Zealand Māori Arts and Crafts Institute:

Watch master carvers and weavers practicing their traditional skills.

Māori Cultural Performances:

Experience Maori storytelling, songs, and the Haka (war dance).

Guided Tours:

Learn about the history and traditions of the area from knowledgeable guides.

Rotowhio Marae:

Explore the meeting grounds of the local iwi (tribe).

Pātaka Kai Restaurant:

Enjoy dining with views of the geothermal valley.

Other Experiences:

Te Pō (Evening Experience):

A magical evening tour with cultural performances, a hangi dinner, and geothermal wonders illuminated at night.

Steambox:

A unique culinary experience where food is cooked using geothermal steam.

Shopping and Dining:

Browse the gift shops for souvenirs and enjoy meals at the restaurant.
